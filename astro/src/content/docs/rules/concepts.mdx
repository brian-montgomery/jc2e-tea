This is an overview of the new concepts and differences introduced in this scenario.

## The Map of America and How to Read It

In this scenario, the firms trade in America, not in India. A number of concepts from the map of
India apply to the map of America, including regions and trade orders. During the China phase, the
procedural flow shifts to the scenario board where trading and military operations happen in America.

<div class="indent">
   ### Regions

   Just like India, the map of America is divided into eight regions, each roughly corresponding to
   a different political and commercial trade center. Each region can be described as the following:

   *  __Colonized regions.__
      Every region has a British flag. When that region's flag is uncovered, it is controlled by the
      British Government and is considered colonized. This term is used to differentiate them from
      the *Company-controlled regions* in India.
   *  __Foreign-settled regions.__
      Regions that are not colonized are considered foreign-settled. These regions will have a
      strength die covering the British flag. Similar to the towers on the map of India, the number on
      the die dictates the strength of the region. This number is used to determine a region's attack and
      defense value. The color of the strength die indicates what foreign power controls those regions.
      _Note: For this print-and-play scenario, it is up to you which colors to use_.
   *  __Allied regions.__
      If two regions in America are either (a) both colonized or (b) have the same color of strength die,
      those regions are allied. _This is mainly used during the event phase._

   ### Ports and the Atlantic Sea Zone

   The east coast of America has three major ports: Boston (**E**), Philadelphia/New York (**W**)
   and Charleston (**S**). All firm ships are associated with the port matching their chosen strategy,
   unless the "Hobnob" strategy is chosen. This applies for both trade and storms.

   When [__Tea Regulation__](/jc2e-tea/rules/viii-parliament-meets/#the-vote-to-regulate-tea) is passed,
   the Company will be able trade in America. For the Company, this is considered to be a single sea zone
   and the three ports are all connected. This means that any player ships used by the Company are considered
   to be in all three ports and thus any Storm rolled on the event die affects the Company ships.

   ### Unrest and Closed Orders

   The trade orders in America may be closed. These closed orders represent smuggling success,
   non-importation agreements against British goods and disputes with the Native Americans.
   Unrest may be added to _colonized regions_, representing political conflict, protests and
   other contention in the Colonies. In terms of gameplay, both of these function similar to
   how they function in India.
</div>

## {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} <Badge text="Clarified" />

:::note
Historically, the British Government placed its Atlantic colonies under the Southern Department
(and later the Colonial Office). To avoid confusion with the term _office_, in this scenario it
is referred to as the _{frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]}_.
:::

The {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} acts similar to a Company Presidency. It maintains its own Military,
offices and tax collectors. These resources are stored in their marked boxes or in the Atlantic Ocean.

<div class="indent">
   ### Hiring

   When hiring, the {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} does not hire family members in the Company
   and vice-versa. Offices in the {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} may hire pensionsers that did
   not retire this turn.

   ### Declaration of War

   Certain events may take place or laws are passed that will cause the Crown to
   [declare war](/jc2e-tea/rules/vii-events/#war-declared-). When this happens,
   officers are more readily hired, the government may take on
   [more debt](#available-credit-and-debt) and conflicts in America are larger.

   ### Board of Trade

   Every turn, the Colonial Department will receive recommendations from the
   [Board of Trade](/jc2e-tea/components/decks/acts/).
   One of these recommendations will be enacted during the Parliament Meets phase, thus changing
   the rules of how the Colonial Department (and perhaps India) operates.
</div>


## {frontmatter._["tea.token.plural"]}

{frontmatter._["tea.token.plural"]} may be acquired during Trade in China, using the export icons and the {frontmatter._["tea.track"]} track. Afterwards, the {frontmatter._["role.company.tea"]} may conduct a Tea Auction, which provides tea to the firms trading in America, for a negotiated price. Any leftover tea are stored in the Company warehouses.

Trading tea allows the firms to get more value out of a trade order in a _colonized region_,
at the risk of rolling less die per unrest.

<div class="indent">
   ### Tea Regulation

   Similar to _Deregulation_ in the 1758 and long 1710 scenarios, the Prime Minister may call
   a special session of Parliament to vote on [Tea Regulation](/jc2e-tea/rules/viii-parliament-meets/#the-vote-to-regulate-tea). Based on the Tea Act of 1773, this gives the Company a monopoly on the
   Tea trade and allows them to compete with the firms and smugglers in trading in America.
</div>


## {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} and Debt

The scenario board has an {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} track, which represents the amount
of capital and borrowing power in the Bank of England.

Whenever debt is removed from Company, {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} or a Firm, raise the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} by that amount. Whenever the Company, {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} or a Firm takes debt, lower the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} by that amount. __If the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} is at 0 and cannot be lowered, only emergency loans and emergency funding may be taken__.

The red cubes are used to track debt in the {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} and firms. The Company uses the Company Debt track on the main board, just like in other scenarios.
