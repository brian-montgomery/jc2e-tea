This is an overview of the new concepts and differences introduced in this scenario.

## America (and Firms)

In this scenario, the firms trade in America, not in India. During the China phase, the procedural flow shifts to the scenario board where trading and military operations happen in America.

<div class="indent">
   ### Colonized Regions

   Any region in America that is controlled by the British Government is a **colonized region**.
   The Colonial flag will be showing in these regions on the map of America. This term is used
   to differentiate them from the Company **controlled regions** in India.

   ### Allied Regions

   If two regions in America are both colonized or have the same color of strength die, they are
   **allied**.

   ### Atlantic Sea Zone

   The east coast of America has three major ports: Boston (**E**), Philadelphia/New York (**W**)
   and Charleston (**S**). All firm ships are associated with the port matching their chosen strategy,
   unless the "Hobnob" strategy is chosen. This applies for both trade and storms.

   When [__Tea Regulation__](#tea-regulation) is passed, the Company can also trade in America.
   For the Company, this is considered to be a single sea zone and the three ports are all
   connected. This means that any player ships used by the Company are considered to be in all three ports and thus any Storm rolled on the event die affects the Company ships.

   ### Declaration of War

   Certain events may take place or laws are passed that will cause the Crown to declare War. When this happens, officers are more readily hired and the government may take on [more debt](#available-credit-and-debt).
</div>

## {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]}

The British Government placed its Atlantic colonies under the Southern Department (and later the Colonial Office). In this scenario, the {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} acts similar to a Company Presidency. It maintains its own Military, offices, tax collectors and --potentially later in the game-- fleet of ships. These resources are stored in their marked boxes or in the Atlantic Ocean.

<div class="indent">
   ### Hiring

   When hiring, the {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} does not hire family members in the Company
   and vice-versa. Offices in the {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} may hire pensionsers that did
   not retire this turn.

   ### Success Checks

   The die rolls in this scenario utilize the same system that the rest of the game uses:
   >  When making a check, first, decide on the amount of resources you want to spend or exhaust,
   >  and gather the appropriate number of dice equal to the amount shown on the office card.
   >  Then, remove dice equal to the penalty. Finally, roll the dice. Use the lowest
   >  rolled value to determine one of three results.
   >  *  1 or 2 is a success.
   >  *  3 or 4 is a failure. **The check may be attempted again if there are sufficient resources**.
   >  *  5 or 6 is a catastrophic failure. The office will take no further actions. The acting
   >     officeholder is immediately returned to its player’s supply, and that office card is
   >     flipped and placed in the Vacant Offices box. Any fatigue that was on the office card
   >     should be returned to the supply.

   However, most rolls in the {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} have other consequences
   associated with rolling 5s or 6s. Therefore, while the lowest rolled value still
   determines whether the check is successful or not, __pay attention to the values on the
   other rolled dice and any consequences that may occur as a result__.

   ### Unrest and Closed Orders

   The trade orders in America may be closed. These closed orders represent smuggling success,
   non-importation agreements against British goods and disputes with the Native Americans.
   Unrest may be added to _colonized regions_, representing political conflict, protests and
   other contention in the Colonies. In terms of gameplay, both of these function similar to
   how they function in India.
</div>


## Tea

{frontmatter._["tea.token.plural"]} are acquired during Trade in China, using the export icons and the {frontmatter._["tea.track"]} track. Afterwards, the {frontmatter._["role.company.tea"]} conducts a Tea Auction, which provides tea to the firms trading in America (for a negotiated price). Any leftover tea is stored in the Company warehouses.

Trading tea allows the firms to get more value out of a trade order in a __colonized region__,
at the risk of rolling less die per unrest.

<div class="indent">
   ### Tea Regulation

   Similar to _Deregulation_ in the 1758 and long 1710 scenarios, the Prime Minister may call
   a special session of Parliament to vote on Tea Regulation. Based on the _Tea Act of 1773_,
   this gives the Company a monopoly on the Tea trade and allows them to compete with the
   firms and smugglers in trading in America.
</div>


## {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} and Debt

The scenario board has an {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} track, which represents the amount
of capital and borrowing power in the Bank of England.

Whenever debt is removed from Company, {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} or a Firm, raise the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} by that amount. Whenever the Company, {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} or a Firm takes debt, lower the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} by that amount. __If the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} is at 0 and cannot be lowered, only emergency loans may be taken__.

The red cubes are used to track debt in the {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} and firms. The Company uses the Company Debt track on the main board, just like in other scenarios.
