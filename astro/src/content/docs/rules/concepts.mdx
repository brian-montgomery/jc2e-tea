This is an overview of the new concepts and differences introduced in this scenario.

## {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]}

The British Government placed its Atlantic colonies under the Southern Department (and later the Colonial Office). In Tea & Business, this department acts like a Presidency that maintains its own Army, offices, tax collectors and --later in the game-- fleet of ships. These resources are stored in their marked boxes or in the Atlantic Ocean.

When hiring, the Company does not hire family members in the {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} and vice-versa.

## {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} and Debt

Whenever debt is removed from Company, {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} or a Firm, raise the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} by that amount. Whenever the Company, {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} or a Firm takes debt, lower the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} by that amount. If the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} is at 0 and cannot be lowered, only emergency loans may be taken.

The red cubes (for the Crown) are used to track debt in for the {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} and firms.

## Trade in America

### Atlantic Sea Zone

The east coast of the 13 colonies is treated as one large sea zone. All firm ships count as if they are in this sea zone, unless the "Hobnob" strategy is chosen.

When the Storm die is rolled, the "4 with no storms" is considered to be the Atlantic sea zone.
