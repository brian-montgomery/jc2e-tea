## Success Checks

Doing business in America is similarly risky to India. Most actions that concern
America require the acting player to make a check to see if their actions are
successful. Each check requires a roll of the dice. The number of dice you roll
is dependent on the resources you have and want to spend as determined by the
action. Any resources spent comes from the office's (or another office's) treasury;
any money is paid to the bank.

The die rolls in this scenario utilize the same system that the rest of the game uses:
>  When making a check, first, decide on the amount of resources you want to spend or exhaust,
>  and gather the appropriate number of dice equal to the amount shown on the office card.
>  Then, remove dice equal to the penalty. Finally, roll the dice. Use the lowest
>  rolled value to determine one of three results.
>  *  1 or 2 is a success.
>  *  3 or 4 is a failure. **The check may be attempted again if there are sufficient resources**.
>  *  5 or 6 is a catastrophic failure. The office will take no further actions. The acting
>     officeholder is immediately returned to its player’s supply, and that office card is
>     flipped and placed in the Vacant Offices box. Any fatigue that was on the office card
>     should be returned to the supply.

:::danger[Pay attention to the entire roll]
Most rolls in the {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} have other consequences
associated with rolling either 5s or 6s. Therefore, while the lowest rolled value
determines whether the check is successful or not, __pay attention to the values
on the other rolled dice and any consequences that may occur as a result__.
:::


## Secretarial Operations

The {frontmatter._["role.govt.governor.full"]} manages the budget for the
{frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} and decides the order in which Trade in
America, military operations and their own actions take place. The firms must
roll first, as is described in
[Filling Orders as a Firm](/jc2e-tea/rules/firms/#filling-orders-as-a-firm).

The {frontmatter._["role.govt.governor"]}, {frontmatter._["role.govt.customs"]}
and {frontmatter._["role.govt.military"]} are not required to take any actions,
but they must be given the opportunity to act. Each position must finish all of
its actions before the next one may begin (players may flip their family members
facedown after to track which have acted).

All of Take Debt, Oversight and Trade in America can be taken anytime during the
{frontmatter._["role.govt.governor"]}'s operations. The {frontmatter._["role.govt.governor"]}
takes a series of Oversight actions using a diminishing dice pool. First,
take dice equal to the number listed on the office card. Then you may take
an Oversight action.

*  __Vacant {frontmatter._["role.govt.governor"]}.__
   If the {frontmatter._["role.govt.governor"]} is vacant, the Prime Minister will
   determine the order of Trade in America  and the {frontmatter._["role.govt.military"]}.
   The Prime Minister acting in this way cannot approve local alliance purchases,
   take debt or take the Oversight action.


<div class="indent">
### Take Debt

A debt can be taken at any time during your turn to add £5 and a red debt cube
to your treasury. This can only be used once per turn (twice if War is Declared).
_Remember to lower the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} by 1._ This action cannot
be taken if the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} is at 0.

### Oversight

Make a check using the number of dice remaining in the pool. There is
no penalty. You may add to this roll by spending £1 from your treasury.

If successful, choose one of the two following options.
*  __Add one military unit__.
   Place a military unit from the supply to the Army of North America.
   Military units are treated like regiments.
*  __Open an Order__.
   Open one closed order in any region in America. If that region is
   _colonized_, add an unrest cube to that region for each 6 that was
   rolled.

Lower the number of dice in the pool by 1 regardless of its result.
You may continue taking actions until one the following happens: you
roll a catastrophic failure, you run out of dice, or you decide to stop.

### Trade in America

The smugglers, firms -- and possibly the {frontmatter._["role.company.tea2"]} --
may fill orders in America during the Trade in America action. The
{frontmatter._["role.govt.governor"]} decides when this action occurs during
their turn, but does not take an active part in it.

Players then fill orders starting with the participant with the lowest initiative
and proceeding to the highest. When it is the smugglers turn, the [Commissioner](#customs)
fills (or closes) the orders on the smuggler cards (if they are not closed already)
and the firm manager (and the {frontmatter._["role.company.tea2"]}, if Tea Regulation
is passed) fills as many orders as possible. Any ties in initiative are decided by
the {frontmatter._["role.govt.governor"]}.
</div>

## {frontmatter._["role.govt.customs.full"]}

The {frontmatter._["role.govt.customs.full"]} may take the Customs action during
the smugglers turn during Trade in America to extract tariffs from the smuggled orders
and raise funds for the Colonial Treasury.

The smugglers will be ordered based on the initiative listed on the current Smuggler
card. The current Colonial Duty will provide the both the base amount of dice to roll
and the amount in tariffs the {frontmatter._["role.govt.office"]} may receive.

<div class="indent">
   ### Customs

   Make a check. Using the current Colonial Duty, determine the default size
   of your dice pool. You may request that the {frontmatter._["role.govt.military"]}
   exhaust military units to add to 1 to your dice pool for each unit exhausted.
   Roll up to that number of dice. _Remember: as with any check, if it is unsuccessful,
   you may attempt it again so long as it did not end in catastrophic failure and you
   have sufficient resources._

   **For each success rolled,** place one collector on one of the marked orders on
   the smuggler card that is not closed on the board. You must place as many collectors
   as possible, but can only place them on orders marked on the smuggler card. For each
   success, increase the Colonial Treasury by the amount listed on the current Colonial
   Duty.

   *  **One Customs Collection.**
      The {frontmatter._["role.govt.customs"]} may only successfully collect customs
      at most once per game turn.

   <div class="bonus">
   Then, you take £1 from the bank for each tariff collected.
   </div>

   <div class="bonus">
   Finally, each player takes £1 from the bank for each of their collectors you
   placed on an order.
   </div>
</div>

## {frontmatter._["role.govt.military.full"]}

The {frontmatter._["role.govt.military.full"]} works similarly to the Commanders
for the Presidents in India. Local alliances may be purchased with permission from the
{frontmatter._["role.govt.governor"]}. Dice are gained by exhausting military units,
officers and local alliances. Dice are lowered by the amount of region strength.
The {frontmatter._["role.govt.military.full"]} does not retire during the London
Season.

The differences with the Deploy action is as follows:
<div class="indent">
   ### Deploy: Peace Accord

   During peace, the {frontmatter._["role.govt.military.full"]} may only deploy in a
   __colonized region__ to open all orders and attempt to clear unrest. Only
   when _War is Declared_ may the {frontmatter._["role.govt.military.full"]} deploy
   in an uncolonized region.

   ### Deploy: Success

   1.  __Take Bonuses.__
      Unlike India, there is no loot to take in America. Instead, the army
      gets a standard military bonus.
      <div class="bonus">
         The {frontmatter._["role.govt.military"]} takes £1 from the bank.
      </div>
      <div class="bonus">
         Each player takes £1 from the bank for each of their surviving officers.
      </div>
   2. __Take Trophies.__
      Take one trophy token for each strength in the uncolonized region
      (since you are the Commander). Then, uncover the flag by the removing the
      region strength die.
   3. __Impose Order.__
      Open all orders in the region. If this was a __colonized__ region,
      remove one unrest cube for each success rolled and add one unrest cube
      for each 5-6 rolled.
</div>
