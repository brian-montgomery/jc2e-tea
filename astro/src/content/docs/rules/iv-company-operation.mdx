## Superintendent of Trade in China: Changes

In this scenario, the Superintendent of China is open at the start. Instead of using the China overlay, use the board on the 1733 board. There is one change to the way that this position works in this scenario:

China has an additional trade option, using Silver.
- The opium trade still gets £4 per export icon, earning the Superintendent a bonus of £1 per export icon. However, it also uses the same number of icons for determining the amount of tea chests.
- The silver trade earns £2 for the company and two tea chests.
- The Superintendent chooses between these two options for trade, earning £1 per export icon either way.

## {frontmatter._["role.company.tea"]}

A new position, the {frontmatter._["role.company.tea"]}, is included.
This position is responsible for managing Tea. It has two steps:

1. Use the {frontmatter._["tea.track"]} to place the amount of {frontmatter._["tea.token.plural"]} to the top of your treasury.
2. Allocate as many {frontmatter._["tea.token.plural"]} in your box to the firms as you would like. Leave any remaining in your treasury.

:::danger

What are the incentives... If the Director of Warehouses just gives the Tea away, the company
doesn't store any up. If there's a requirement that a firm must pay at least £1 per crate,
Tea isn't profitable enough for the firms.

:::
