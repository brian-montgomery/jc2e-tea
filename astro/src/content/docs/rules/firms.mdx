For the duration of the scenario, the firms operate in the American mainland, not India. The **India Treasury** space holds revenue gained from trade in America (instead of India). This treasury is used to pay dividends. Any remainder is then transferred to the London treasury.

:::note
The law to deregulate **is not present** in this scenario. However, the rules regarding deregulation are present from the beginning, with the following modifications.
:::

## The Firms Phase

After the Family phase is complete and before the Hiring phase, players will now perform the Firms phase. The Firms phase has two steps: Firm Investments and Firm Strategy.

## Firm Investments

The firm investment phase has the following changes:

<div class="indent">
### Open Bank Loan

If a firm needs additional capital, the firm's manager can choose to take a loan from the Bank. For each bank loan taken:
*  Place a red cube in firm's Shares box.
*  Decrease the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} by 1.
*  Add £5 to the firm's London Treasury.

A bank loan counts as a share in regards to expenses and the ten shares limit. **A red cube in the Shares box may not be traded.**

### Close Bank Loan

A firm's manager may choose to close a loan from the Bank. For each bank loan to be closed:
*  Pay £5 from the firm's London Treasury.
*  Remove a red cube from the firm's Shares box.
*  Increase the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} by 1.
</div>

## Firm Strategy

All firms in this scenario will trade in America, not India. When choosing a strategy, a firm is not deciding what sea zone they will be placed in, but which port they are going to initiate their trade at.

<div class="indent">
### Tea Auction

During the tea auction, a firm may acquire {frontmatter._["tea.token.plural"]}, using the
funds in their Trade Bid space. Therefore, a firm manager should place any funds needed for
both the tea auction and trade when deciding on their strategy.

### Firms and Storms

When rolling for storms during the Events in America phase, treat all firm ships as
if they were in the area indicated by the firm's strategy. However, if the firm's chosen
strategy was “Hobnob,” that firm's ships will not be affected by storms.
</div>

## Filling Orders as a Firm

At the start of a {frontmatter._["role.govt.governor"]}'s Operations (before any actions are taken),
all firms reveal their strategy and **declare any regions they wish to trade Tea in**. Make a check with dice equal to:
*  The amount of cash allocated to the strategy.
*  The amount of {frontmatter._["tea.token.plural"]}, minus the amount of unrest cubes in the declared regions.

The cash is then paid to the bank and the {frontmatter._["tea.token.plural"]} should be kept. If at least one success
is rolled, the firm may trade. Each firm that successfully trades has an initiative value equal to the total of their
ships minus the number of successful dice (1s and 2s) they rolled. This number can be negative.

Orders are filled after the {frontmatter._["role.govt.customs"]} has made a roll while taking the
Customs action (and the {frontmatter._["role.company.tea2"]} if Tea Regulation is passed). The active
smuggler card determines their initiative.

   *  **Vacant {frontmatter._["role.govt.governor"]} or Failure to Trade.**
   If the {frontmatter._["role.govt.governor"]} is vacant, ties in initiative
   are decided by the Prime Minister. Firms will fill orders
   after the Commissioner and Commander have had a chance to act.

Players then fill orders starting with the smugglers, firm or Company with the lowest initiative
and proceeding to the highest. When it is their turn, the Commissioner or firm manager
trading fills as many orders as possible. Any ties in initiative are decided by the
{frontmatter._["role.govt.governor"]}.

Firms fill a number of open orders up to the number of ships they own **plus one**.
Orders must be filled starting with the associated port listed on their strategy. When
filling an order, you must be able to trace a path back to this port through any
number of orders that you have filled.

   *  **Previously-Filled Orders.**
      Firms may fill orders previously filled this turn by other firms, smugglers or the Company.
      The Company may fill orders previously filled by firms or smugglers this turn. The filling
      of a previously-filled order will generate half of its listed value, rounding down.

   *  **Trading with Tea.**
      As a firm with {frontmatter._["tea.token.plural"]} remaining is filling orders, it may
      spend one at an order within its declared regions. Spending a {frontmatter._["tea.token.singular"]}
      will generate an additional half of the order's listed value, rounding down.

      _Tea may be traded at an order that is already filled_. After all orders have been filled, any
      remaining {frontmatter._["tea.token.plural"]} should be discarded.

## Firm Expenses and Dividends

A firm pays expenses and dividends, with the following additions.

<div class="indent">
### Firm Expenses

Each firm must pay for the following expenses:
* Pay £1 for each ship on the firm's charter.
* Pay £1 for each share **and bank loan** in the firm's Share box or pay £ equal to the firm value,
whichever is higher.

### Emergency Bank Loans

A firm facing dissolution may acquire an emergency Bank Loan. Treat this the same as an Emergency Investment, using a red debt cube instead of a family marker. Decrease the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} by 1. _If a firm is at the ten shares limit, a debt cube is still added to the Shares box._

### Firm Dividends

When paying dividends, the Bank must be paid £1 per red cube in the Shares box. The amount paid to the bank **does not count** towards the firm's special retirement amount.
</div>
