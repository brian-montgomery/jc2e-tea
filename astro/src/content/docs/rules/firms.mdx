:::danger[Warning]
Please be familiar with the firm rules in the __Deregulation__ scenarios first. We strongly recommend that all players experience on the scenarios with firms before attempting to play this scenario.
:::

The law to deregulate is not present in this scenario. However, the rules regarding deregulation are present from the beginning, with the following modifications:

## Firms

For the duration of the scenario, the firms operate in the 13 Colonies, not India.

The **India Treasury** space holds revenue gained from trade in America (instead of India). This treasury is used to pay dividends and expenses. Any remainder is then transferred to the London treasury.

## The Firms Phase

After the Family phase is complete and before the Hiring phase, players will now perform the Firms phase. The Firms phase has two steps: Firm Investments and Firm Strategy.

### Firm Investments

The first step of the Firms phase has no set order and players can take the following actions at any time during it. The Firms phase ends when no player wishes to take further actions. Any timing conflicts related to a firm are resolved by the firm’s manager. Any timing conflicts between firms are resolved by the Prime Minister.

<div class="indent">
#### Court and Workshop Investment

As in the original scenarios, players may invest in a firm with the consent of the firm's manager.

#### Bank Investment

If a firm wishes investment and no players have interest in investment, the firm's manager can instead choose to take investment from the Bank. For each bank investment taken:
*  Place a red cube in firm's Shares box.
*  Decrease the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} by 1.
*  Add £5 to the firm's London Treasury.
</div>

### Firm Strategy

All firms in this scenario will trade in America, not India. Thus when choosing a strategy, a firm is not deciding what sea zone they will be placed in, merely which port they are going to initiate their trade at.

### Firms and Storms

When rolling for storms during the Events in India phase, treat all firm ships as
if they were in the Atlantic sea zone. However, if the firm's chosen strategy was
“Hobnob,” that firm's ships will not be affected by storms.
