### Closed/Filled Orders

There are 17 pieces used to mark closed orders in America and those filled by the
Company or firms. These function the exact same as in India. _You can either print
additional tokens from the John Company print and play, or use coins as a substitute_.

### Regiments and Local Alliances

There are {frontmatter.metadata.militaryUnits} military unit and
{frontmatter.metadata.localAlliance} local alliance pieces which strengthen
the British army in North America. The military units represent the Colonial
Militia, British Army and Navy. The local alliances represent the different
Native American nations that the British allied with during various conflicts.

### Speculation Tokens

Used similarly to loot tokens in the base game, the {frontmatter.metadata.speculation}
speculation tokens indicate how much wealth is available when certain regions in
America are colonized. _Because these tokens aren't ready, we recommend using
8- or 10-sided dice as a substitute._


## Needed Components and Dice

_{frontmatter._["game.title"]}_ uses several different types of pieces to track the
various entities in America. Since this is a print-and-play scenario, you will need
to provide your own additional components:
-  2 pawns for track markers.
-  About {frontmatter.metadata.pokerChips} poker chips.
-  {frontmatter.metadata.sixSidedDie} 6-sided die of 4 different colors.

### Pawns and Cubes

You will need two additional pawns for the {frontmatter._["tea.track"]} and
{frontmatter._["credit.track"]} tracks on the scenario board. The 12 red cubes
from the base game are used to represent debt.

### {frontmatter._["tea.token.plural"]}

Tea gets produced by trading in China and is auctioned to the firms
for trade in America. To represent the {frontmatter._["tea.token.plural"]},
we recommend using poker chips. You will probably need a maximum of
{frontmatter.metadata.pokerChips}, although less is needed if you choose
to use different denominations.

### Region Strength

Similar to the towers in India, there are flag spaces on the map that
represent whether a region is colonized by Britain. When it is not,
a 6-sided die represents the military strength of that region. To show
regions that are aligned (or allied), use dice of the same color. Because
different colors are needed, our suggestion is to have 4 different
colors amongst {frontmatter.metadata.sixSidedDie} dice.
