When setting up this scenario, follow the usual rules for setup (on page 4 of the manual),
but use the following instead of step 10:

<div class="bold-list">
10. Place the scenario board next to the main board.
    You may place this on the left of the main board to better represent geography,
    or to the right of the main board to better incorporate game flow.
11. Shuffle the Events in America cards and place them near the Indian Event tiles.
12. Place the Native American local alliance tiles on the *Army of North America*
    space.
13. Place a marker on the "S" space on the {frontmatter._["tea.track"]} track
    and on the highest space on the {frontmatter._["credit.track"]} track.
14. Place the additional colored six-sided dice next to the Scenario board.
15. Place the additional office, smuggler and {frontmatter._["deck.laws.singular"]} cards nearby.
16. Place the {frontmatter._["tea.token.plural"]} where they are accessible.
17. Place each speculation token in the matching region in America (£ side face **down**).
18. Place a black pawn on "2" space on the Turn track, and the arm on the Prime Minister dial
    to point to the "Social Tax plus Window Tax" space halfway in between the 1710 and 1758
    starting spaces.
19. Use the Scenario Cards. Follow any instructions on the front of the cards, taking components
    as needed and using pawns to mark the listed position on various tracks. Then, set up the regions
    of India and America as shown on the back of the cards (and also [listed below](#the-india-and-america-maps)).
20. Use the Setup Cards.
</div>

## The India and America Maps

         #### Closed Orders
         *  All orders in ___Punjab___ and ___Hyderabad___ are closed.
         *  The two southernmost orders in ___Delhi___ are closed (£3 and £5).
         *  The southernmost orders in ___Maratha___ (£2), ___Madras___ (£4), and ___Mysore___ (£6) are closed.

         *  ___Maratha___ has a strength of 2.
         *  ___Punjab___ has a strength of 0.
