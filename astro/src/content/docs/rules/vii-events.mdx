After resolving Events in India, roll the storm die, resolve any storms and then
resolve a number of events in America during which the political and economic
landscape of America will shift and the Colonial Department may face reaction
from the Colonies.

## The Storm Die

Roll the storm die. The resulting face indicates which ships are subject to storms
and how many events occur in America.

- **Storms in America.** If one of the three sea ports are rolled, roll a die for each
ship in that port, except Company and Extra ships. Treat all firm ships as if they were
in the area indicated by the firm's strategy.

- **Events in America.**  For each event in America, flip one card from the event deck.
These events will often open or close orders, add unrest to a region, and may lead to
Cascades. **The location of an event often depends on the back of the top card in the
Events in America draw stack and/or on the most recently passed
{frontmatter._["deck.laws.singular"]}.**

<div class="indent">
   ### Collector Loss

   When resolving events, orders sometimes become closed. If a collector is
   on such an order, the collector is returned to its owner's supply. If the
   order has a filled order token, flip it instead.

   ### Cascade

   Follow the same rules as listed in the manual for a Cascade in India (_If
   all orders are closed, first try connecting orders, than northernmost open
   orders, than trigger an additional Cascade_).
</div>

## Region Loss

Attacks against the Colonies can trigger the loss of a colonized region. When
this occurs, follow these steps:

1. __Tarnish the Commander's Name.__
   The Commander of North America returns half (rounding up) of the trophies
   their family owns to the supply _e.g. a player with 3 trophies would lose 2._
   Return the Commander to its supply and place its office card in the Vacant Offices
   box.
2. __Officer Rout.__ Roll a die for every officer in the Army and remove it if
   the roll is a 6.
3. __Establish Authority.__
   Remove any unrest in the region and place a strength die (of the appropriate
   color) on the flag in the region with a value of 1 (unless otherwise noted).
   Close every open order in the region, resolving a Cascade if all are already closed.
4. __{frontmatter._["role.govt.governor"]} resignation.__
   If two or more regions are lost in the same turn, the
   {frontmatter._["role.govt.governor.full"]} is removed from office. Place its
   card in the Vacant Offices box and the associated player loses a victory point.
   _Place the family marker in Debtor's Prison to represent the point lost._
5.

## Event Concepts

When resolving the Events in America, there are a couple of terms to understand.

### {frontmatter._["event.movement"]}

When resolving an event, you may need to perform a {frontmatter._["event.movement"]}.
_This is a simplified version of the Elephant's March._

1. Move the Bear to the region shown on the top of the draw stack.
2. Place the Bear on the border matching the shape (circle, triangle, or square)
   printed on the event card to show the tension. If the bear is facing an allied
   region (_either both regions are colonized or have the same color of region strength
   die_), rotate the Bear clockwise from that border until it is not.
   *  **Fully-Allied.** In the rare instance that all neighbors are allied, move the Bear
      across the border to the region matching the shape and repeat step 2.
   :::note
   The bear will always be on a border pointing from one region to another.
   :::

### Impacted Regions

When resolving an event, you may need to know what the Impacted Regions are.
On almost all of the [{frontmatter._["deck.laws.plural"]}](/jc2e-tea/components/decks/acts),
the section at the bottom is a list of regions that are affected by its passing.
The impacted regions are the _colonized regions on the most recently passed_
{frontmatter._["deck.laws.singular"]}.

   *  **No Impacted Regions.** There are a couple of {frontmatter._["deck.laws.singular"]} cards
      that do have any impacted regions. In this case, there are no impacted regions for this turn.
   *  **Tea Regulation.** The Tea Regulation card does not have any impacted regions and
      should be ignored when determining the most recently passed {frontmatter._["deck.laws.singular"]}.

## Events Described

All of the events in America are described in the following sections.

<div class="indent">
   ### Protest

   First, add an unrest cube to each impacted region.

   Next, locate the region on the top of the draw stack.
   If that region is _impacted_, perform [Correspondence](#correspondence).
   Otherwise, if that region is colonized, add an unrest cube to it.

   <div class="indent">
      #### Correspondence
      Locate the colonized region(s) with the most amount of
      unrest. Add an unrest cube to each adjacent colonized region
      with less unrest. _Do not add more than one unrest to each
      region._
   </div>

   ### Dispute

   Locate the region pictured on the top of the draw stack. If it's colonized,
   close the westernmost open order. Otherwise, close the easternmost open order.
   Any collector on the order is returned to that player's supply.
   If all orders in this region are already closed, perform a Cascade.

   ### Boycott

   Locate the region pictured on the top of the draw stack. If it's impacted,
   close all orders in the region. If it's colonized, add an unrest cube to
   the region. Otherwise, add one strength to the region.

   ### Peace
   First, perform a Bear Crawl with the shape on the card.

   Next, open any orders that are connected through the border
   the Bear stands on. Add one strength to each foreign-settled region
   touching the Bear. Remove half of the unrest (rounded down) from each
   colonized region touching the Bear.

   ### Conflict

   A Conflict is similar to a Crisis in India. It is the most common event
   and resolves one region attacking another. The size and scope of a Conflict
   will depend on whether [War is Declared](/jc2e-tea/rules/concepts/#declaration-of-war).

   First, perform a Bear Crawl with the shape on the card. The tail
   of Bear indicates the attacker and the head of the bear indicates
   the defender.

   <div class="indent">

      #### Aggression

      If the attacker is a colonzied region, resolve Aggression. The
      strength of the attack is the amount of unrest plus the modifier
      listed on the event card. During Aggression, the defender's strength
      is just the strength of the region (ignore any allied regions). If
      the attacker's strength is greater, the Aggression is successful;
      otherwise, the Aggression fails.

      If the Aggression is successful, open any connected order in the
      defender's region. If there are no connected orders of if the order
      is already open, open the easternmost closed order in the defender's
      region instead. Lower the strength of the defender's region by 1,
      to a minimum of 1.

      {/* TODO: Determine whether to roll a die here to see a reaction. */}
      *  **Forced Migration.**
         For each region adjacent to the defender that is foreign-settled,
         increase its strength by 1.

      If the Aggression failed, close any connected order in the attacker's
      region. If there are no connected orders of if the order is already closed,
      close the westernmost closed order in the attacker's region instead.
      Perform a Cascade, if necessary.

      #### Raid

      If the attacker is a foreign-settled region, resolve a Raid. In a Raid,
      the attack is only between the attacking and the defending region
      (ignore any allied regions). Determine whether the attacker's strength
      plus the modifier listed on the event card is greater than the defender's
      strength. If so, the Raid is successful; otherwise it fails.

      When a Raid is successful, the defender is now allied with the attacker.
      Change its strength die to match the attacker. Then, close every open order
      in the defending region. If all are already closed, resolve a Cascade.

      If the Raid failed, lower the attacking region's strength by 1 (to a
      minimum of 1).

      #### Attacks against the Colonies

      A Raid against a colonized region is handled similarly to attacks against the
      Company in India.

      -  **Attack Strength.**
         The attack strength is the strength of attacking region plus the modifier
         on the card. If [War is Declared](/jc2e-tea/rules/concepts/#declaration-of-war),
         also add the strength of any allied regions of the attacker.

      -  **Mounting Defense.**
         The {frontmatter._["role.govt.military"]} must exhaust pieces in the Army
         equal to the strength of the Conflict or as many as possible. If the
         newly-exhausted pieces do not equal the strength of the Conflict, the
         Conflict succeeds. Perform the steps of [Region Loss](#region-loss).

         If the attack failed, remove unrest in the defending region _up to the strength
         of the attack_. The {frontmatter._["role.govt.military"]} should take a trophy
         token. Lower the strength of the attacking region by one (to a minimum of one).

      -  __Vacant Commander.__
         If the {frontmatter._["role.govt.military"]} is vacant, the
         {frontmatter._["role.govt.governor"]} will act in its stead (choosing which
         pieces to exhaust). If that office is also vacant, the Prime Minister will
         choose. The player acting as Commander will not lose or gain trophies.

      -  __Declaring War.__
         After the Conflict has been resolved, regardless of the outcome, flip the
         Colonial Decree to the _War Declared_ side.

      -  __Scenario End.__
         If after the attack, there are no more colonized regions, trigger the end of
         the scenario.
   </div>

   ### Foreign Invasion

   Roll the storm die and resolve a Raid and/or Attack against the Colonies in each
   region linked to a storm (_New England_ for the East, _Delawarean_ for the West, and
   _Southern Provinces_ for the South). If all storms are rolled, resolve this event once
   in each. If no storms are rolled, resolve this event in the region on the top card
   of the draw stack instead.

   For each region that a Foreign Invasion is happening, follow the rules for a Raid or
   Attack against the Colonies (depending on the defender), with the following changes:
   -  __Declare War.__
      If the defender is a colonized region, immediately flip the Colonial Decree to
      the _War Declared_ side.
   -  __Foreign Neighbor.__
      If the defending region has an adjacent region that is not allied, treat the
      attack as originating from the strongest non-allied adjacent region.
   -  __Attack Strength.__
      Roll a six-sided die. The attacking strength will be the greater of the die roll
      and the strength of the strongest _foreign neighbor_ (including their allies).

   In addition to those rules, if the Foreign Invasion is successful:
   -  Close every open order in the region. If all orders are already closed, perform a Cascade.
   -  If there was a foreign neighbor, match the color of the die when covering the flag.
      Set the region's strength to half the strength of the Invasion (round down).

   ### Revolt

   A rebellion occurs in each colonized region with unrest. Each colonized region's strength
   is their amount of unrest. Follow the rules for an Attack against the Colonies. __If there
   are no regions with unrest, you may ignore this event and reshuffle this card.__

   Before any of the rebellions begin, you must do the following:
   -  __Declare War.__
      If the number of rebelling colonies is greater than one, immediately flip the
      Colonial Decree to the _War Declared_ side.
   -  __Check for Militia.__
      For each military unit in the Army of North America, roll a die. On a roll
      of 5 or 6, discard that unit and add an unrest to the rebelling region with
      the greatest amount of unrest.
   -  __Local Alliances.__
      With any money currently in the Colonial treasury, the {frontmatter._["role.govt.governor.full"]}
      may allow the {frontmatter._["role.govt.military"]} to purchase local alliance(s).

   If any of the rebellions succeed, consider the revolution successful. Follow the steps for
   [Region Loss](#region-loss) for each region involved. Cover the flags of the rebelling regions
   with the same colored die and set each region's strength to half of their attacking strength
   (rounded down).

   If all of the rebellions fail, the {frontmatter._["role.govt.military"]} gains a Trophy for
   each region. Open the easternmost closed order and remove all unrest from each region.

   __Afterwards,__ if there are no more colonized regions remaining, trigger the end of the scenario.
   Otherwise, shuffle this card back into the event deck.

</div>
