## The Storm Die

Roll the storm die. The resulting face indicates which ships are subject to storms
and how many events occur in India.

-  **Storms.** If one of the three sea zones is rolled, roll a die for each ship in
that sea zone except Company and Extra ships. If the die face with three
storms is rolled, you must check for all sea zones. If the die face with no storms
is rolled, you must check all ships in the Atlantic (the sea zone next to America).

- **Events in America.** After resolving the storms and events in India, resolve as
many events in America as indicated by subtracting the the number on the Storm die from 5.
For each event in America, flip one card from the event deck. These events will often open or
close orders, add unrest to a region, and may lead to Cascades. **The location of an
event often depends on the most recently passed {frontmatter._["deck.laws.singular"]}.**

:::note

Because of how this works, the following is true.
- You will always be rolling for storms in at least one sea zone in every Event phase.
- Between India and America, you will be resolving a total of five events in every Event phase.

:::

### Firms and Storms

When rolling for storms during the Events in India phase, treat all firm ships as
if they were in the Atlantic sea zone. However, if the firm's chosen strategy was
“Hobnob,” that firm's ships will not be affected by storms.
