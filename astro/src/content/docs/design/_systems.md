## Firms in America

### Overview
- Function like "traditional" firms
- Starts with retired family member
- Starts with shipyard (ship in firm)




Trade
- British Firms trade like normal
   - Tea gives extra money at extra risk.
- Commissioner tries to catch foreign trade

Colonies
- Want trade/tea
- Westward Expansion (?)
   - Land Companies

First Lord
- Selects Trade Acts
- Gets 1-2 votes (as compensation)

Colonial Secretary
- Envoy: Opens trade routes
- Brings over Military (Navy/Army)
- Hires local alliances (Militia and Tribes)
- Can take debt

Commander-in-Chief
- Deploys Military

Tax Collectors
- One position OR
- Equivalent to writers
   - Pro: Don't retire.
   - Pro: Provides hiring to First Lord and Colonial Secretary.
   - Secretary rolls (just like President).
   - Collectors marked filled orders, just like writers
      - Can use generic writer tokens.
   - Cap on bandwidth of writers, just like in EIC.
   - One (or Two) die rolls on stopping.
   - Power is in perception of the Tariff (and how effective it might be).
   - Gives danger to the position (Revolt or something similar)
   - Gives Colonial Secretary more power.
   - Colonial Secretary gets fired if his administration disatrously fails.
